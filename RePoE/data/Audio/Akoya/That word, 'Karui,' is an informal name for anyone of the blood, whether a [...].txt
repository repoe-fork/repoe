That word, 'Karui,' is an informal name for anyone of the blood, whether a chieftain, a child, or a <i>{kaivana}. The Ngakuramakoi, however, are those who truly comprise our culture. They must be followers of the Way, and they must have passed their test of adulthood, at the Isle of Shrikes or elsewhere. A great warrior does not have to be Karui by blood to become Ngakuramakoi. It is rare, but it does happen. I shall be watching you with great interest.
